What actually changed, and why

  • Palette: indigo-to-purple gradient (the single most recognizable "AI made this" signal) vs. a palette generated fresh for this project's mood, verified against the contrast contract before it shipped.
  • Logo: a letter in a gradient box vs. a constructed geometric mark (a coffee bean), the same rule as any letter-in-a-box logo the checklist flags.
  • Icons: emoji standing in for icons vs. real fetched icons from a set matched to the mood, tinted to the accent.
  • Sections: three identical cards, heading plus two sentences vs. a visual step-by-step, an asymmetric lead feature with a real stat, and a stat row, show-don't-tell instead of a text wall.
  • Motion: a fully static page vs. a sequenced hero entrance and scroll reveals, reduced-motion-aware.
  • Type: default line-height on a 44px headline vs. tightened leading on the display type, a real heading/body pairing instead of one font doing both jobs.

Both pages are real, live HTML, not mockups. ← back to tastemaker
